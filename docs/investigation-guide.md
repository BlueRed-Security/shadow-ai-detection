# Shadow AI Investigation Guide

This guide helps security analysts investigate alerts generated by Shadow AI detections, including:
- Network-based AI usage
- Local LLM execution
- AI-related extensions

---

## Investigation Objectives

When a detection is triggered, the goal is to determine:

- Is the AI usage **authorized or unauthorized**?
- Is there a **risk of data exposure**?
- Is the activity **isolated or persistent**?
- Does it indicate **policy violation or insider risk**?

---

## Step 1 — Identify the Context

Start by gathering basic information:

- User: Who initiated the activity?
- Device: Which endpoint is involved?
- Time: When did the activity occur?
- Frequency: One-time or repeated behavior?

---

## Step 2 — Network-based AI Usage

### Key questions:
- What AI service was accessed? (ChatGPT, Copilot, Gemini, etc.)
- Is the service approved by the organization?
- How frequently is the user accessing it?

### Investigate:
- RemoteUrl
- Connection frequency
- Time patterns (working hours vs off-hours)

### Risk indicators:
- High frequency connections to AI services
- Access from non-technical users
- Access combined with large data uploads (if available)

---

## Step 3 — Local LLM Usage

### Key questions:
- What tool is being used? (Ollama, LM Studio, etc.)
- What command was executed?
- Is this expected for the user role?

### Investigate:
- ProcessCommandLine
- FileName
- Parent process (if available)

### Risk indicators:
- Execution of `.gguf` models
- Repeated use of `ollama run` or `llama.cpp`
- Usage on non-developer endpoints

---

## Step 4 — AI Extensions

### Key questions:
- What extension was installed or modified?
- Is the extension approved?
- Which platform is used? (Browser, VS Code, JetBrains)

### Investigate:
- FileName
- FolderPath
- ActionType (FileCreated, FileModified)

### Risk indicators:
- Installation of AI extensions by non-developers
- Multiple AI tools installed on the same endpoint
- Recent installation followed by AI network usage

---

## Step 5 — Correlation (VERY IMPORTANT)

The most valuable insights come from combining signals:

### Example scenarios:

#### High Risk
- Local LLM execution + `.gguf` files
- AND frequent AI network traffic
- AND AI extensions installed

 Indicates active AI usage with potential data processing

---

#### Medium Risk
- AI extensions installed
- AND occasional network access

Likely usage, needs validation

---

#### Low Risk
- Single network connection
- No local execution
- No extensions

 Possibly benign or accidental usage

---

## Step 6 — Decision Making

### Ask:

- Is this activity aligned with company policy?
- Is sensitive data at risk?
- Is this expected behavior for this role?

---

## When to escalate

Escalate if:

- Sensitive data may have been exposed
- Unauthorized tools are actively used
- Repeated behavior is observed
- Activity involves privileged users

---

## Recommended Actions

Depending on the case:

- Inform the user about policy
- Block access to AI services (if required)
- Restrict extension installation
- Monitor the endpoint more closely
- Initiate data exposure assessment

---

## Analyst Tips

- Do not assume malicious intent — many users are unaware of risks
- Focus on **behavior + context**, not just detection
- Always correlate multiple signals before concluding

---

## Final Note

These detections provide **visibility, not verdicts**.

Proper investigation and context are essential to determine:
 risk  
 intent  
 impact
